Fried Rice

Fried rice is my favorite dish to both make and eat. Such a simple dish elevated by ingredients considered to be "cheap".

To make fried rice you will cook 1-2 day old rice in a wok over very high heat. Add aromatics and frozen vegatables, alongside a scrambled egg to add brightness and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cup - day-old rice
  • 3 tbs - grapeseed oil (or other neutral oil)
  • 2 tbs - soy sauce
  • 1 egg - scrambled
  • 1 tbs - minced ginger
  • 1 tbs - minced garlic
  • 12 cup - frozen vegetables
  • 1 scallion - thinly sliced (optional)

Steps

  1. In a wok, heat 1 tbs grapeseed oil over high heat until shimmering
  2. Add the egg and cook for ~1 minute, stirring constantly. Transfer to a small bowl.
  3. Heat 1 tbs until shimmering and add the rice.
  4. Immediately swirl 1 tbs of soy sauce to around the edge of the wok and begin to coat the rice.
  5. Allow the rice to cook and form a slight crust before turning again. Repeat until the rice has an even crust and is no longer sizzling.
  6. Form a whole in the center of the rice and add the last tbs of oil. When hot, add the aromatics and frozen vegetables, stirring constantly for ~30s.
  7. Add the egg and the last tbs of soy sauce and continue stirring the rice for another 1-2 minutes.
  8. Remove from heat and serve immediately. Garnish with sliced scallions and season to taste with additional soy sauce.
